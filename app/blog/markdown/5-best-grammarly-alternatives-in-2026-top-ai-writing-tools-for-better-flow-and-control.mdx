Grammarly alternatives are surging in 2026 as writers look for tools that go beyond basic grammar checks to deliver real-time rewriting, tone shifts, paraphrasing, and true customization—especially on mobile. If app-switching, rigid suggestions, or limited mobile support frustrates you, these 5 options stand out for smoother workflows and more control.

### 1. ProWritingAid – Best for In-Depth Style and Long-Form Editing
ProWritingAid excels where Grammarly often feels surface-level. It provides 25+ detailed reports on style, readability, overused words, pacing, and more—ideal for authors, bloggers, and professionals polishing essays or reports.  
While strong on desktop with integrations for Word and Google Docs, its focus is deeper analysis rather than instant mobile rewriting. Great if you want comprehensive feedback before finalizing.

### 2. QuillBot – Best for Paraphrasing and Quick Rewrites
QuillBot shines as a paraphrasing specialist with modes like "Standard," "Fluency," "Creative," and "Formal." It includes a solid grammar checker and summarizer, making it popular for students, content creators, and anyone needing to rephrase text fast without losing meaning.  
It offers a keyboard on some platforms, but its strength lies in browser extensions and the web editor for targeted sentence-level changes—less seamless for full mobile typing compared to dedicated keyboards.

### 3. Wordtune – Best for Sentence-Level Refinement and Tone Tweaks
Wordtune focuses on rewriting individual sentences or short paragraphs with options to make text shorter, longer, more casual, or professional. It feels intuitive for emails, social captions, or quick edits, often producing more natural-sounding variations than basic tools.  
Available via browser and apps, it helps punch up short-form content effectively but may require more manual selection than always-on keyboard solutions.

### 4. LanguageTool – Best Multilingual Grammar and Accessibility
LanguageTool supports over 20 languages with strong grammar, style, and punctuation checks—plus an AI rephrase feature in premium. It offers a live iOS keyboard, desktop apps, and browser extensions, making it one of the closest "everywhere" alternatives for non-English writers or global teams.  
It emphasizes correctness and affordability over creative rewriting, but its keyboard integration reduces friction for mobile proofreading.

### 5. RewriteMate – Best AI Keyboard for Seamless Mobile Rewriting on iOS
RewriteMate stands out as a true system keyboard extension for iOS, living directly in your typing area across any app—no switching to ChatGPT or separate editors required. Select text, tap one-tap commands (Proofread, Paraphrase, Tone Change, Expand/Shorten, Translate), or use the "Glass Box" editor to customize prompts and build personal presets (e.g., "Make it professional but keep my original voice" or "Add Gen-Z tone with hashtags").  
This transparency and zero-friction approach eliminate the copy-paste cycle, making it ideal for everyday chats, work emails, social media captions, or support replies. Privacy-focused: only selected text is processed, with no storage or training use.

### Why Consider These Grammarly Alternatives in 2026?
Grammarly remains trusted for quick red-lines and corrections, but many users hit limits on mobile (context loss, generic outputs, app friction). These tools address that by offering:
- Deeper customization and control (RewriteMate's editable prompts, ProWritingAid's reports)
- Specialized strengths (QuillBot for paraphrasing, LanguageTool for multilingual)
- Better mobile experiences (in-keyboard rewriting without interruptions)

If you're on iPhone and want an evolution beyond basic fixes—especially one that works everywhere with your own voice—testing an in-keyboard option like RewriteMate could cut your writing time significantly.
